Mar 10 3:00 p.m. 5:00 p.m. SS 1253

Inquiry and Metacognitive Feelings

Authors have argued that it is inappropriate to inquire into a question whose complete answer one already knows, and to hold the interrogative attitudes that frequently accompany inquiry toward such a question. I draw on the psychological literature on metacognitive feelings, in particular the ‘feeling of knowing’ (FOK), to show that there are many cases in which it is perfectly appropriate in inquire into a question whose answer one already knows.
